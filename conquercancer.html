Riding to
Conquer Cancer

Last year I had the privilege of watching my dad complete the Ride to Conquer Cancer in Toronto. After battling cancer himself, and beating it, he took to one of the biggest challenges of his life, riding 200 km over two days to raise money to fight the disease that threatened his life.

Seeing him complete this feat inspired me. By the end of the first day I promised myself I'd be by his side the next year and we'd take it on together. I've known so many people affected by cancer, and many who have lost their lives. It's time to conquer cancer.

I couldn't believe how emotions just came in. I know how I felt when my doctor said "it looks really good". I went back to my car, and I just sat there and bawled. That was the first time I had felt like a survivor.

-Steve Rae
