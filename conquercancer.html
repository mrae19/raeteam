Riding to
Conquer Cancer

Last year I had the privilege of cheering my father on to complete the Ride to Conquer Cancer in Toronto - a 200km ride from Toronto to Niagara. It was an emotional event, seeing thousands of riders take to the course to ride for a cure to the disease the threatened my fathers life, and had taken many others.

Witnessing him take on this challenge made me incredibly proud, and it was a significant milestone in his recovery from cancer. I promised myself that the next year, I would be riding side by side as he crossed the finish line. I'm overly excited to ride with my Dad in the 2018 Ride to Conquer Cancer.

Neither of us enjoy asking for financial support, but this is truly a cause that has affected our family, friends and co-workers, and has likely had an impact in your life as well. If you can, we'd greatly appreciate your support. Learn More.

I couldn't believe how emotions just came in. I know how I felt when my doctor said "it looks really good". I went back to my car, and I just sat there and bawled. That was the first time I had felt like a survivor.

-Steve Rae
